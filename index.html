A Man Ahead of His Time

The Artist Within The Genius

Leonardo DaVinci was an Italian artist, scientist, and inventor born in 1452 in Vinci, Italy. He was a true Renaissance artist best known for painting the Mona Lisa and The Last Supper. He was also an engineer known for inventions like the flying machine and the crossbow. His natural genius crossed so many disciplines that he epitomized the term. Leonardo pushed the boundaries with his innovative creativity.

"Simplicity is the Ultimate Sophistication" - Leonardo DaVinci

Fun Fact:

So nobody could steal his ideas, Leonardo DaVinci invented a secret way of writing in his journal; the only way to decipher the notes was with a mirror. It makes you wonder what other secrets DaVinci wasn’t ready to reveal. After all, he was ahead of his time; perhaps he felt the world wasn’t prepared. It was even rumored that DaVinci sabotaged one of his weapon machines that could someday destroy any country’s enemies. Still, he thought something this powerful might fall into the wrong hands and never finished the machine.
